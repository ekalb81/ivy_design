High-Fidelity Test Results

To recap, I tested users on three different red routes for the gardening app during the user interviews. I wanted to make sure the flows made sense to users and were intuitive. The goal was to eliminate as many pain points as possible.

  1. Save an item to their favorites and access the favorites page.
  2. Add a comment to a post in the community forums.
  3. Add an item to the calendar.

I conducted these tests in person and online via Zoom. I began by introducing myself and asking the users some preliminary questions to get them comfortable. I helped them become familiar with the dashboard of the app and told them about our goals. I proceeded to give them the tasks while encouraging them to think out loud. Two rounds of user testing were conducted. The first round had 4 participants and the second round had 5 participants. 

Problems & Solutions

These are some of the most common pain points that occurred during the first round of user tests. I brainstormed and implemented solutions into the prototype and did a second round of user tests. 

Problem The floating action button on the dashboard was a particularly obvious pain point. Several users would click it first when attempting to do the first task, which was not the correct course of action. 

Solution The floating action button on the dashboard was removed, and the task boxes that it was initially associated with gained their own buttons to perform the same function. 

Problem The speech bubble icon that indicated the number of replies on a post within a forum was initially not clickable, as it was just meant to indicate how many people had interacted with a specific post. Several users attempted to click it in order to perform the second task.

Solution I made the speech bubble icon on posts clickable so that it would lead the user to the same page that clicking the rest of the preview image would.

Problem Pop-up boxes had to be manually dismissed by users. This was cumbersome during the tests and it would abrupt their flow when performing the given tasks.

Solution I made the pop-up boxes automatically dismiss themselves after a short period of time

Problem It was unclear to some users that the bookmark button next to the plant images in that plant profile page was meant to save it to their save library. 

Solution I labeled the bookmark button in order to make the functionality clear, so there was no guesswork on the user’s end.
